Key Findings

Transfer Bandwidth: KoalaBear H2D peaks at ~54 GB/s with pinned memory (3x faster than pageable ~19 GB/s). BLS12-377 H2D peaks at ~17 GB/s (AoS→SoA transpose overhead). D2D copies reach ~1.9 TB/s internal bandwidth.
NTT Performance: KoalaBear NTT at 16M elements: 2.1 ms (31.8 GB/s effective). BLS12-377 NTT at 4M: 3.2 ms (41.3 GB/s effective). End-to-end (H2D+FFT+D2H): KoalaBear 16M in 8.7 ms, BLS12-377 4M in 21.6 ms — dominated by PCIe transfers at large sizes.
GPU vs CPU Speedup: BLS12-377 FFT: 15.9x (16K) to 58.2x (1M). KoalaBear vector multiply: 50x-283x at large sizes. Vortex commit: 7.2x (4K×128) to 13.7x (64K×512) — limited by H2D copy of raw rows.
MSM Performance: Pippenger MSM peaks at ~1.2 GB/s (scalar throughput) at 1M points, 17.3 ms per MSM. Scales to 128M points (2.5 s) using chunked two-pass approach to fit sort buffers in VRAM.
Batch NTT: Amortized kernel launch overhead via batch API: 100 × 256K NTTs in 1.68 ms (62.3 GB/s effective) vs 7.2 ms for 100 individual calls — 4.3x improvement from batching.
Poseidon2: Batch compression (width=16) at 65K hashes: embedded in the Vortex commit pipeline. GPU Vortex commit for 64K×512 matrix: 13 ms total (RS encode + SIS + Poseidon2 + Merkle tree).
